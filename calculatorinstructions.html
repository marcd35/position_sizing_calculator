Method 1: Total Account Risk %

Use this when you want to risk a fixed percentage of your account on a trade. The calculator converts that risk into a maximum share count based on your stop loss.

  1. Enter your Account Value.
  2. Enter Total Account Risk % (e.g., 1%).
  3. Enter Entry Price and Stop Loss.
  4. Optional: set Max Positions to limit per-position allocation.
  5. Click Calculate.

Back to top

Method 2: Dollar Amount Risk

Use this when you prefer to define risk as a fixed dollar amount (e.g., “I’m willing to lose $100”). The calculator finds max shares based on the difference between entry and stop.

  1. Enter your Dollar Risk.
  2. Enter Entry Price and Stop Loss.
  3. Optional: enter Account Value to see position size as a percentage.
  4. Click Calculate.

Back to top

Method 3: Position % Allocation

Use this when you want to size a trade based on allocating a percentage of capital to a position. The calculator converts the allocation into max shares, and still shows risk-per-share.

  1. Enter your Account Value / Allocation Amount.
  2. Enter Position Risk % (or use presets/slider).
  3. Enter Entry Price and Stop Loss.
  4. Click Calculate.
